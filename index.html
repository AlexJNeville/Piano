Alex Neville


Pianist, Cellist, Accompianist

Me and Piano

Playing the piano has been a significant part of my life, a journey marked by achieving a distinction in Grade 8 and earning a Level 4 diploma. Beyond these accomplishments, accompanying choirs and delving into a wide range of repertoire has deepened my connection to music. For me, the piano isn't just an instrument; it's a way of expressing myself, a constant in my life through ups and downs, and a bridge to others. My dedication to piano has shaped me, blending discipline with emotional expression in a way that feels both personal and universal. It's not just about the notes and performances, but the joy and growth I've found along the way.

The art of cello

Playing the cello has woven deeply into the fabric of my identity, enriching my life beyond measure. My experiences with orchestras and ensembles have been invaluable. Each rehearsal and performance has been a step in a journey of emotional and musical growth. The cello for me is not just an instrument; it's a profound means of expression, a way to convey feelings and stories that words cannot capture. Being part of an orchestra has taught me the power of collaboration, the beauty of blending my voice with others to create something larger than ourselves.

Accompanying

I am truly privileged to have had the opportunity to accompany an eclectic mix of choirs, extending from harmonious vocal ensembles to captivating solo instrumental performances. This journey has been an extraordinary symphony of collaboration, where each note and melody weaves a unique tapestry of sound, elevating the musical experience to new heights. Engaging with such diverse musical talents has not only been a profound honor but a deeply enriching adventure into the heart of musical expression.
