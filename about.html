About Me


My Journey

Beginnings

I started playing piano from the age of 5 taking in school lessons, and making steady progress, enjoying every moment of it. After a couple of years learning in Worcester my family moved to Malvern, where I had piano lessons with my remaining teacher Tim Sidford, an outstanding pianist with a collection of awards, achievements and even the honour of playing infront of the late queen. Around the same time I started playing the cello, and instrument that I am not as comfident at, but still play in an array of orchestras, such as the Worcestershire youth orchestra, as well as being invited to the National School Symphony Orchestra, and participating in the Youth English Symphony Orchestra. All moments which I cherish. When I was 12, lockdown hit and we were all thrown inside, and my piano also ground to a halt, as the inability of being able to go to lesson lessened motivation for both the piano and cello, and both were halted momenteraly.

Achievements

As Lockdown came to a thankful end, my piano restarted, and I quickly finished grade 6 piano- performance, I skipped grade 7 and moved on to grade 8, which I got a decent distinction in, all under the age of 15. In 2023 at the age of 15 I was awarded my ARSM performance piano diploma, an award which im over the moon about, as I believe the dedication it took to get here
was heavily worth it. Since then I have had the opportunity to play both piano and cello in musicals, and In multiple orchestras and concerts. I am now looking to play piano in restaurants, carehomes or other occasions, to be able to spread the joy that I get from playing the piano.
